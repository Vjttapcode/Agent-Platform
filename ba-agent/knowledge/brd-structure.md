# Business Requirements Document (BRD) — Standard Structure

A BRD draft should contain these sections (omit a section only if truly N/A,
and say so):

1. **Document control** — version, date, author, status.
2. **Executive summary** — 3–5 sentences: the problem and the proposed solution.
3. **Business objectives & success metrics** — measurable goals (KPIs).
4. **Background / problem statement** — current situation and pain points.
5. **Scope**
   - In scope
   - Out of scope
6. **Stakeholders** — table of role, interest, influence.
7. **Functional requirements** — table of `FR-###`, description, priority (MoSCoW).
8. **Non-functional requirements** — table of `NFR-###`, category, target.
9. **Assumptions & constraints**
10. **Risks & mitigations** — table of risk, impact, likelihood, mitigation.
11. **Dependencies**
12. **Open questions** — `Q-###` list of unresolved items.
13. **Appendix / glossary** — domain terms.

Keep the draft realistic: when the user has not supplied enough detail, fill
gaps with clearly-labelled **Assumptions** and list the unknowns under
**Open questions** rather than inventing certainty.
