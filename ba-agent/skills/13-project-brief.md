# Skill: Project Brief (BMAD Analysis artifact)

**When:** the user asks for a project brief, or the BMAD **Analysis** phase runs.
This is the foundational living document that feeds the PM's PRD.

**Produce a single, clean Markdown document** with these sections:

1. **Title & one-liner** — the product in one sentence.
2. **Problem statement** — the pain, who has it, and why it matters now.
3. **Goals & success metrics** — measurable outcomes (reuse the Success Metrics
   skill); name a North-Star metric.
4. **Target users & stakeholders** — primary/secondary users, buyer, others.
5. **Proposed solution (summary)** — the approach at a high level, not a spec.
6. **Scope** — In scope / Out of scope (be explicit about what's deferred).
7. **Key assumptions** — labelled, reviewable.
8. **Risks** — table: risk · impact · likelihood · mitigation.
9. **Open questions** — `Q-###` list to resolve before/with the PM.

**Rules:**
- Fill genuine gaps with clearly-labelled **Assumption:**; never invent certainty.
- Keep it concise but complete — this is read by humans and used downstream.
- This brief is the **handoff to Planning (PM → PRD)**; end with a short
  "Ready for PRD?" checklist of what's still needed.
